Dragon Roll Delight

1. Rinse the sushi rice in a strainer until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes and then drain.

2. Cook the rice in a rice cooker or on the stove according to the package instructions.

3. While the rice is cooking, mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small saucepan and heat until the sugar dissolves.

4. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl and add the vinegar mixture. Mix the rice gently and thoroughly, being careful not to break the grains.

5. Cover the rice with a damp cloth and let it cool to room temperature.

6. Prepare your sushi fillings by slicing them into thin pieces.

7. Lay a sheet of nori seaweed shiny side down on a bamboo mat or a piece of plastic wrap.

8. Wet your hands with water to prevent the rice from sticking to them. Spread a thin layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top.

9. Arrange your sushi fillings horizontally on top of the rice.

10. Roll the sushi tightly using the bamboo mat or plastic wrap to help you. Press firmly to seal the roll.

11. Slice the sushi roll into bite-sized pieces using a sharp knife.

12. Serve the sushi with wasabi paste, soy sauce, and pickled ginger on the side.

Dragon Stone Idli

Dragon Roll Delight

1. Rinse the idli rice and urad dal separately, and soak them in separate bowls with enough water to cover them for at least 4-5 hours or overnight.

2. After soaking, drain the water from the rice and grind it into a fine paste using a wet grinder or a blender. Add water little by little as needed to help the rice grind smoothly. Set aside the rice batter in a large bowl.

3. Now, grind the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds to a smooth and fluffy batter. It may take around 10-15 minutes depending on the quality of the dal. Add water little by little to help with the grinding process.

4. Mix the urad dal batter with the rice batter, adding salt to taste. Mix well using your hand or a spoon to form a smooth batter. The batter should be slightly thicker than dosa batter, but not too thick.

5. Cover the bowl with a lid or plastic wrap and keep it in a warm place to ferment for 6-8 hours, or overnight. The batter will rise and become slightly fluffy.

6. Grease the idli moulds with oil or ghee, and fill them with the fermented batter. Steam the idlis in a steamer or idli cooker for 10-15 minutes or until they are cooked through.

7. Once the idlis are cooked, remove them from the moulds using a spoon or a knife. Serve hot with chutney, sambar, or any other side dish of your choice.

Dragon's Fire Snowflake Blini

Dragon Roll Delight

1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, and salt.

2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, and vegetable oil.

3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. The batter will be thick and slightly lumpy.

4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Add a small amount of butter or oil to the pan.

5. Using a spoon or ladle, drop small amounts of batter onto the pan, making pancakes about 2-3 inches in diameter.

6. Cook until the surface of the blinis is covered with small bubbles and the edges begin to dry out, then flip the blinis over and cook until the other side is lightly browned.

7. Remove the blinis from the pan and repeat until all of the batter is used up.

8. Serve the blinis warm, with toppings such as sour cream, smoked salmon, caviar, or fruit preserves.

Enchanted Dragon Bansh

1. In a mixing bowl, mix the minced meat, chopped onion, salt, and pepper together until well combined.

2. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut it into circles.

3. Place a spoonful of the meat mixture in the center of each dough circle.

4. Fold the dough over the meat and pinch the edges together to seal.

5. Bring water to boil in a large pot.

6. Place the bansh in a steamer basket and steam for about 15 minutes, until the dough is cooked through.

7. In a separate pan, heat some oil and lightly fry the cooked bansh until they turn golden brown.

8. Serve hot and enjoy!
